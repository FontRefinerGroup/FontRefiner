Abstract

The complex topology and aesthetic properties of Chinese characters confer both substantial artistic freedom and engineering challenges in typeface design. In modern font production, manual drafting and automated tools often introduce severe geometric redundancy to closely match visual targets. This not only increases storage and rendering overhead, but also impedes downstream editing due to topological disorder. To address font curve optimization, we propose FontRefiner, a multimodal large model for Bézier curve sparsification of Chinese glyphs. FontRefiner supports two complementary glyph refinement paradigms, few shot reference guided refinement and zero shot universal refinement, and enables end to end glyph image vectorization and reconstruction. To effectively integrate the refinement task into an MLLM, we parameterize vector glyphs as SVG XML and elevate the task from local geometric fitting to structured code rewriting, allowing the model to learn designer decision priors. To overcome the multi template context bottleneck, we transform SVG text into visual signals and leverage the encoder’s semantic compression to produce extremely low dimensional dense representations. Architecturally, we adopt a dynamic resolution vision encoder with a hybrid attention mechanism, and inject hierarchical visual features into the language decoder via multi scale feature projection to mitigate geometric information degradation in autoregressive generation. To advance this line of research, we build CFRefiner 2M, a dataset of 300 fonts with two million non canonical to canonical glyph pairs. To our knowledge, this is the first work focused on Bézier curve sparsification and structural refinement for Chinese glyphs. With a three stage progressive training strategy, FontRefiner achieves state of the art performance and shows strong potential for integration into professional Chinese typeface design workflows.

Experiment

Visualization of vector refinement analysis on manually created pseudo-characters. Even when the input character is a linguistically nonexistent pseudo-glyph, the model still exhibits strong structural generalization ability.

Visualization of reference-template sensitivity analysis. When partial discrepancies exist between the reference characters and the input character, the model demonstrates robustness to the reference templates.

Experiments on complex calligraphic and artistic typefaces show that FontRefiner can preserve the visual aesthetics of highly variant glyphs while reconstructing geometrically compact and topologically optimal structures.

Visualization of stroke-level unordered topology perception. The original reference characters are decomposed into independent stroke paths and randomly permuted, thereby disrupting their global structural information. Despite the reference templates degenerating into a collection of disordered stroke fragments, FontRefiner can still effectively exploit these unordered fragments as geometric cues to guide the reconstruction of non-canonical glyphs.

Vector refinement on glyphs synthesized by few-shot font generation methods. The input images are raster glyphs produced by a few-shot font generation model and exhibit unsmooth contours. While preserving the original glyph structure and style, FontRefiner reconstructs high-quality vector glyphs with smooth outlines and geometrically compact representations.

Qualitative evaluation of cross-lingual zero-shot generalization. Although FontRefiner is trained only on a Chinese-character dataset, it demonstrates strong generalization to unseen writing systems (Latin letters, Korean, and Japanese). The model learns language-agnostic vector design priors, enabling it to effectively handle character sets with diverse geometric properties.

Zero-shot generalization results on vector icons. Although FontRefiner is trained only on Chinese glyph data, the implicit geometric priors it learns transfer effectively to general planar vector graphics. While substantially optimizing the topology, the rendered visual appearance remains strictly consistent.

Qualitative experiment on vector glyph optimization based on disordered component references.

Elimination of generative artifacts. While the input raster images contain floating noise produced by generation models, FontRefiner effectively filters out these non-semantic artifacts during reconstruction, producing clean and usable vector glyphs.

Visual verification of radical consistency. For identical radicals appearing across different characters, FontRefiner generates geometrically consistent and standardized structures.

Qualitative results of structural defect restoration. FontRefiner successfully identifies and corrects these topological defects, restoring the glyphs to their canonical forms based on learned structural priors.
